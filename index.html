Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Who We Are

Oxbridgerton is a private members' society based in London. This policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Data Controller

Oxbridgerton is the data controller for the personal data we collect. For any data-related enquiries, please contact us at contact@oxbridgerton.com.

What Data We Collect

When you submit a membership application, we collect:

  • Name, email address, and date of birth
  • Location
  • University, degree, and professional information
  • LinkedIn profile and Instagram handle (where provided)
  • Referral code (where provided)
  • Free-text responses about your achievements
  • Photographs you upload

When you submit a nomination, we collect your name and email, along with the nominee's name, email, university, a description of why you are nominating them, and any photographs you upload.

Why We Collect It

We process your personal data for the purpose of assessing membership applications and managing our members' society. Our lawful basis for processing is consent, which you provide by ticking the consent checkbox on our application or nomination form.

How We Store Your Data

Your data is stored securely using Google Firebase (Firestore for text data and Firebase Storage for photographs). Firebase servers are operated by Google and data may be stored in the United States. Google operates under appropriate safeguards for international data transfers, including Standard Contractual Clauses.

How Long We Keep Your Data

If your application is successful, we retain your data for the duration of your membership. If your application is unsuccessful, we retain your data for up to 6 months following our decision, after which it is permanently deleted. Nomination data is retained for the same periods.

Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third parties for marketing purposes. Your data is accessible only to the Oxbridgerton membership committee for the purpose of assessing applications. Our data is hosted on Google Firebase infrastructure.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Withdraw your consent at any time
  • Request restriction of processing
  • Data portability — receive your data in a structured format
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk

To exercise any of these rights, please contact contact@oxbridgerton.com. We will respond within 30 days.

Cookies

Our website does not use cookies for tracking or analytics purposes. Firebase may use strictly necessary cookies for service functionality.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.
